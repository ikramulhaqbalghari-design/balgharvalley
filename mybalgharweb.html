GILGIT BALTISTAN

.

Gilgit Baltistan is a stunning mountainous region in northern Pakistan, famous for its breathtaking landscapes, snow-capped peaks, lush valleys, and pristine rivers. The region offers a unique blend of natural beauty and cultural heritage.

Its major attractions include majestic mountains like K2, serene lakes such as Attabad and Sheosar, and historical sites including forts and ancient mosques.

Capital: Gilgit
Borders: Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Afghanistan, China.

GHANCHE DISTRICT

Ghanche is the easternmost district of Gilgit-Baltistan, rich in cultural and historical beauty. Chaqchan Mosque and Khaplu Palace reflect traditional craftsmanship.

Cultural dances like Mandoq Taimo and Ragi Taimo are popular.

  • Headquarter: Khaplu
  • Languages: Balti, Urdu, English
  • Activities: Trekking, Camping, Fishing

KHAPLU FORT

Khaplu Fort locally known as Yabgo Khar (meaning "The fort of Doqsa"), is an old fort and palace located in Khaplu, district Ghanche. It was built in the mid-19th century, replacing an earlier fort located nearby. It served as a royal residence for the Raja of Khaplu.

The architecture of Khaplu Fort is a fusion of Himalayan, Kashmiri and Mughal styles. The spaces inside the palace provide a historical record of the raja's lifestyle.

The architectural style reflects cultural influences of Mughal and Kashmiri traditions. The natural surroundings enhance its beauty and heritage value.

One of the most stunning features of the Fort is its semi-octagonal timber entrance which transforms into a balcony on the second floor, and a guest room on the third floor.

ABOUT GHANCHE DISTRICT

GHANCHE DISTRICT

.

Ghanche Map


Ghanche is the easternmost district of Gilgit-Baltistan, rich in cultural and historical beauty. Chaqchan Mosque and Khaplu Palace reflect traditional craftsmanship.

Cultural dances like Mandoq Taimo and Ragi Taimo are popular.

  • Headquarter: Khaplu
  • Languages: Balti, Urdu, English
  • Activities: Trekking, Camping, Fishing

Etymology

.

The word "gang" in the Balti language means glacier, and "che" is used to indicate abundance. The term is used by the residents of the Khaplu Valley for the Ghanche Nallah, a seasonal stream flowing through Khaplu during summer. When the government upgraded the area to district level, the name Ghanche was officially adopted.

Geography

.

Ghanche is the easternmost district of Gilgit-Baltistan and one of the coldest regions of Pakistan, often called the "third pole". The Shyok River flows through the district, while Khaplu Valley and Hushe Valley serve as gateways to the great Karakoram Mountains.

The region includes world-famous peaks such as K2, Broad Peak, Gasherbrum, and Masherbrum. Much of the land is snow-covered, with limited forest areas.

History

.

Until 1820, Ghanche was ruled by the Yabghu dynasty, which had close ties with the Maqpon dynasty of Skardu. Later, Ahmad Shah of Skardu annexed the region, followed by the Dogra rule in 1840.

After the 1947 Gilgit Rebellion, Ghanche became part of Pakistan. The district was officially established in 1974, later reorganized, and regained its district status in 1989.

BALGHAR

Balghar Valley

BALGHAR VALLEY:

Balghar (Urdu: بلغار وادی) is a village situated in Ghanche District, Gilgit Baltistan, Pakistan, along the banks of the Shyok River.

The confluence of the Shyok River and the Indus River occurs to the west of Balghar. The valley includes multiple neighborhoods (mahallahs), forming a diverse and culturally rich community.

  • Total Mahallahs: 15 (Gond Balghar, Rotika, Ongbo, Marmyoung, Louhra, etc.)
  • Nearby Valleys: Daghoni (East), Karis (West), Shiger (North), Kharfaq (South)
  • Distance: 30 km from Ghanche HQ, 110 km from Skardu
  • Elevation: 2,635 meters above sea level
  • Agriculture: Main occupation of local people

MILITARY CONTRIBUTION

Kargil War
Tamgha-e-Jurat
Balghar has played an important role in the Pakistan Army. Abdul Qadir Shaheed was awarded Tamgha-e-Jurat during the 1999 Kargil War.

ANJUMNA-E-TALBA BALGHAR

Non-governmental organization (NGO)
Anjuman-e-Talba Balghar is a non-governmental organization working for the welfare of the local community. It focuses on highlighting public issues and presenting them to the concerned authorities to ensure solutions.

The organization plays an important role in solving basic problems such as electricity issues, water supply, and other community needs, helping improve the living conditions of the people of Balghar.
